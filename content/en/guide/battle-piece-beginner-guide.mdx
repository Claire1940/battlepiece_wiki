<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Farming and Leveling Mechanics">

Leveling up efficiently in Battle Piece requires understanding where to farm and how to maximize your experience gains. Bandit farming forms the foundation of your early progression loop, allowing you to learn the nuances of combat while steadily increasing your level. As you approach higher levels, transitioning to structured activities becomes necessary to maintain a steady flow of experience points.

The progression loop can be broken down into specific activities, each tailored to different stages of your character's growth.

| Activity | Purpose | Player Action | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bandit Farming** | Early Leveling | Defeat low-level bandit groups repeatedly | Levels 1 to 100 |
| **Quest Line Progression** | Mid-Game EXP | Complete NPC tasks across different islands | Levels 100 to 400 |
| **Private Server Grinding** | Lag Reduction | Farm bosses without public server latency | High-ping players |
| **Cooperative Raids** | End-Game Loot | Team up to clear waves of high-level enemies | Levels 400 to 600 |
| **PvP Sparring** | Combat Practice | Fight players in controlled matches | Level cap preparation |

Using a private server is highly recommended for grinding. Public servers often suffer from congested spawns and high latency, which can disrupt your attack combos and increase the time it takes to defeat mobs. In a private server, NPC spawns are predictable, and your inputs will register with minimal delay.

<Callout type="success" title="Farming Efficiency">
Grouping up multiple bandits before unleashing your area-of-effect (AoE) skills is the fastest way to clear quests. Pull three to four enemies together, line up your attacks, and defeat them simultaneously to maximize your EXP per minute.
</Callout>

When participating in PvP, focus on spacing. Many players make the mistake of running directly at their opponents, making their attacks easy to predict and counter. Instead, bait out your opponent's primary cooldowns, dodge their initial strikes, and punish them during their recovery frames.

<Callout type="warning" title="Raid Coordination">
Raids are significantly harder to complete solo. Enemies spawn in large waves, and bosses have massive health pools. Always try to enter raids with a balanced team consisting of at least one crowd-control specialist and two high-damage dealers.
</Callout>
